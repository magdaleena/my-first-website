The best cafes for freelancers in London
Where can you be most productive?

You’ve finally kicked the office habit and have started working freelance - congrats! That said, you’re probably looking for that perfect spot where you can plug your laptop, get some reliable wifi and focus on your work while sipping on a delicious coffee. Here are some best spots in town:

Federation Coffee

££££ Cafe/Bakery in Soho

Are you in central or just love the heart of London so much? If you have a couple of hours to spare between meetings or simply love the location (and friendly prices) you should definitely visit this spot. This coffee basically exists to serve laptop crowds. There are plenty of tables and sofas but it is popular so make sure you come early to reserve a seat. Oh… and did we mention food? You can get anything from a salad to a chocolate fondant, in case you have more of a sweet tooth (but hey, we don’t judge!).

Lots of tables and comfy sofas

Great coffee

Wide selection of food and snacks

Instagram picture friendly

Can get crowded

Address: 77-78, Brixton Village and Market Row Markets, Brixton, London SW9 8PS

Website: federation.coffee

The Riding House Café

££££ Cafe/Bakery in Fitzrovia

Just because your Instagram bio says ‘digital nomad’ now doesn’t mean that you can’t get cabin fever from working in dingy cafes. Hit the Riding House Cafe in central which is a lovely and incredibly convenient place to post up for a few hours. The sofas and long communal table are a good shout, and it’s also bright inside, which helps if you’re prone to a bit of seasonal affective. There’s a varied all-day menu of posh brasserie dishes, but it’s also good for eggs in the morning too. Bear in mind that it gets busy at peak times.

Vegan friendly (dairy-free milk selection)

Reliable WiFi

Very spacious

Cheap and cheerful

Address: 43-51 Great Titchfield St, London W1W 7PQ

Website: ridinghouse.cafe

Bean and Hop

££££ Cafe/Bar in Earlsfield

If we’re talking incentives to get work done, a cold craft beer must be up there. Bean & Hop in Earlsfield is a cafe by day that does excellent sausage rolls and good coffee, but has an extensive craft beer list that you can get through at night. The internet’s reliably fast and there are plenty of power outlets, and the cafe has the sort of laid back indie vibe that’s always welcome in this part of town.

Reliable WiFi

Lots of power outlets

Great coffee (and drinks!)

Can get noisy

Address: 424 Garratt Ln, London SW18 4HN

Website: beanandhop.co.uk
